Chicken

A chicken is a domesticated bird commonly raised on farms for its meat and eggs, known for its characteristic clucking sounds and often having a variety of colors like white, brown, or black; male chickens are called roosters and are larger with more vibrant plumage, while females are called hens and lay eggs; both sexes have fleshy skin folds on their heads called combs and wattles, and they are social animals living in flocks with a pecking order hierarchy to establish dominance; chickens are omnivores, foraging for food by scratching the ground with their feet, and are unable to fly long distances due to their relatively heavy bodies and short wings

Beef

Beef cattle are cattle raised for meat production (as distinguished from dairy cattle, used for milk production). The meat of mature or almost mature cattle is mostly known as beef. In beef production there are three main stages: cow-calf operations, backgrounding, and feedlot operations.

Sushi

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish consisting of seasoned vinegared rice, usually topped with raw seafood, vegetables, or cooked egg, and often wrapped in a sheet of seaweed (nori), making it a popular food that features the rice as the main component, not just the raw fish as many people might think; the word "sushi" itself translates to "sour rice" due to the vinegar used in the rice preparation.
